At Eden, our main product was a report platform where doctors write diagnostic reports for X-rays and other studies. 
Product wanted to build a template system so doctors could reuse common report texts instead of writing from scratch every time.
The initial design treated templates as full report replacements. You pick a template, it fills the entire report. 
As I was developing it, some questions arose around usage and corner cases. Like, what if the doctor had a template for describing a vertebrae fracture but wanted to keep their own introduction? It would overwrite everything.
The design changed from full-report replacement to blocks that could be inserted into specific sections of the report. 
Product took that back to the client, validated it, and we went back and forth a couple more times.
We ended up with templates as reusable blocks that could be either inserted into specific parts of the report or be the whole report.
It matched much better how doctors actually write reports.
